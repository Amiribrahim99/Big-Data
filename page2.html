Importance of Big Data

Big data collected in their systems are used by companies to improve operations, provide better customer service, create personalized marketing campaigns based on specific customer preferences and ultimately increase profitability. Businesses that utilize big data hold a potential competitive advantage over those that don't since they're able to make faster and more informed business decisions, provided they use the data effectively.
For example, big data can provide companies with valuable insights into their customers that can be used to refine marketing campaigns and techniques in order to increase customer engagement and conversion rates.
Additionally, the use of big data allows companies to become increasingly customer-centered. Historical and real-time data can be used to determine customers ' changing tastes, thereby allowing businesses to update and develop their marketing strategies and become more sensitive to customer wants and needs.
Health researchers also use big data to determine risk factors for diseases, and physicians to better diagnose diseases and disorders in individual patients. Additionally, data from electronic health records (EHRs), social media, the web, and other sources provide up-to-the-minute information about threats or outbreaks of infectious diseases to health care organizations and government agencies.
Big data helps oil and gas companies identify potential drilling locations and monitor pipeline operations in the energy industry; likewise, utilities use this to track electrical grids. Financial services companies use big data systems to manage the risk and analyze market data in real time. Manufacturers and carriers are dependent on big data to monitor their supply chains and automate distribution routes. Many applications of government include emergency response, fighting violence, and smart city initiatives.
